Earth in depth

Earth is the only planet confirmed to support life, thanks to its balanced temperature, protective atmosphere, and liquid water. It also hosts a dynamic climate system that shapes continents, weather, and biodiversity.

Mars in depth

Mars once had rivers and lakes, and scientists believe it may have harbored microbial life in the distant past. Its thin atmosphere and cold climate make it one of the most promising targets for future exploration.

Saturn in depth

Saturn is a gas giant with a massive system of rings and many moons. The planet’s atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, and its rings are made of icy fragments that continue to reveal details about the early solar system.
